Kaly Lynn Beede

I am a senior studio artist, emphasizing in sculpture at Arkansas State University. The act of celebrating the unity, love, and emotion of life is the center of my artistic expression, and inhabits everything I create. I work primarily with florals and the abstract, using organic shapes, vintage color schemes, and found objects that play with themes of self-worth and connection.. My experiences, hardships, breakthroughs, relationships and observations are what shape the messages that I want to share with the world. Ultimately my art is about truly reaching the viewer; I want to provide them with my own truth and vulnerability so that they feel more comfortable and confident in their own.

Patty Nall Scott

I am a local studio artist with an emphasis in Sculpture at Arkansas State University. I also have an interest in Printmaking and Ceramics. Sculpture allows me to work with a wide array of mediums; paint, clay, wood, glass, yarn, wire, plastics and found objects. The message I am trying to convey will determine the medium used. This past summer, I created a series in printmaking (screen and linoleum prints), using the domestic crocheted doily as it transcended through four generations of women in my family. Other works have been inspired by life experiences dealing with loss, health, emotional and social issues, while others have been created to recognize, honor or in memory of a person or profession. And lastly, I have created pieces inspired by nature such as the oldest tree on the ASU campus still finding life and purpose in spite of its old age and broken limbs. In addition to my studies at ASU, I have attended the International Glass Artist conventions in Las Vegas, Nevada in two consecutive years, taking classes under a master glass mosaic artist from Australia.
